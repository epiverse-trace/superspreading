# superspreading: Understand Individual-Level Variation in Infectious Disease Transmission

Estimate and understand individual-level variation in transmission.
Implements density and cumulative compound Poisson discrete distribution
functions (Kremer et al. (2021)
[doi:10.1038/s41598-021-93578-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-021-93578-x)
), as well as functions to calculate infectious disease outbreak
statistics given epidemiological parameters on individual-level
transmission; including the probability of an outbreak becoming an
epidemic/extinct (Kucharski et al. (2020)
[doi:10.1016/S1473-3099(20)30144-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1473-3099%2820%2930144-4)
), or the cluster size statistics, e.g. what proportion of cases cause
X% of transmission (Lloyd-Smith et al. (2005)
[doi:10.1038/nature04153](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature04153) ).
